📖 How to Use FamilyFinance
FamilyFinance — Personal Finance Hub
Dashboard — Your Financial Overview

The Dashboard is your home screen. It shows:
• Financial Readiness — A mini arc gauge in the top bar scores your overall financial health (savings, debt, credit, budget surplus) at a glance, visible whenever you're on the Dashboard.
• Goal Countdown & Financial Goals — One combined card: your #1 priority goal countdown on the left (with a savings progress bar for amount-based goals), and your full goals list on the right.
• Key Stats — Four cards in a 2×2 grid: Total Savings, Total Debt, Monthly Surplus, and Lowest Credit Score.
• This Month's Bills Summary — Quick view of bills for the current month.

First steps: Go to Settings and enter your name, birth date, and income. Then add bills, budget items, and debts.
Goals — Your Priority-Ranked Financial Roadmap

The Goals section is the heart of your financial plan. Your #1 priority goal drives the dashboard countdown timer and is displayed prominently every time you open the app.

Key process flow:
1. Click + Add Goal and choose a goal type: Target Date, Net Worth, Savings Amount, Monthly Income, Emergency Fund, or Custom.
2. Give your goal a name, a description (shown on the dashboard), a target date, and an amount if applicable.
3. Use the ▲ ▼ arrows in the ranked list to reorder your goals — whatever is #1 drives the countdown.
4. Update your Current Progress amount on savings-type goals to see a progress bar fill up.
5. Click ✓ Mark Complete when you hit a goal — it moves to the completed section and the next goal takes the countdown.

Goal types explained:
• 📅 Target Date — a deadline-based goal with no dollar target (e.g. "Be credit card free by Dec 2027")
• 💰 Net Worth — tracks total assets minus liabilities toward a number
• 🏦 Savings Amount — tracks a specific savings target with a progress bar
• 💵 Monthly Income — tracks progress toward a monthly income level
• 🛡 Emergency Fund — specialized savings goal for your safety net
• ✏️ Custom — anything you want to track
Bill Tracker — Track Monthly Bills & Income

Key process flow:
1. Click + Add Bill to enter each recurring bill (rent, utilities, subscriptions, etc.).
2. Set the bill's frequency (Monthly, Weekly, Biweekly, etc.), amount, and due date.
3. Check bills off as paid using the checkbox on each row. Multi-occurrence bills (e.g., weekly) track a count.
4. Click 💰 Income Sources to manage your income streams. Salary and Commission sources take a frequency and start date — pay entries appear automatically each month. Gig / Freelance and Other sources are saved as named templates only; use + Log Income to record each payment as it arrives.
5. Click + Log Income to record any payment on the spot. If you have Gig sources set up, a Quick Pick dropdown lets you select the source name instantly. Enter the date, amount, and an optional note.
6. All income appears as green rows alongside bills in the tracker, sorted by date.
7. Use 📋 Copy → Next Month to carry recurring bills forward. When used in December, the next year is added to the selector automatically.
8. Use ⬇ Export XLS to download a spreadsheet with all bills and income for the year.
9. Use ⬆ Import CSV to bulk-import bills (required columns: Name, Amount, Category).
10. Use 📦 Archive Year at year-end to preserve records.
Budget Planner — Know Where Your Money Goes

Key process flow:
1. Click + Add Income to enter your income sources with frequency (biweekly, monthly, etc.).
2. Click + Add Expense to enter your monthly spending categories with budgeted and actual amounts.
3. Use 📋 Seed from Bills to auto-import your Bill Tracker items as budget expense lines — great for a quick start.
4. The Monthly tab shows income vs. expenses with a surplus/deficit view and spending by category.
5. The Medium Term tab tracks 1–5 year financial targets (emergency fund, debt payoff, etc.).
6. Click 🏦 + Savings Fund to create named savings buckets (Emergency Fund, Vacation, Down Payment) and track your progress toward each goal.
Debt Eliminator — Pay Off Debt Strategically

Key process flow:
1. Click + Add Debt to enter each debt (credit cards, auto loan, student loan, etc.) with balance, interest rate, and minimum payment.
2. The Overview tab shows all debts ranked by payoff priority with projected payoff dates.
3. The ❄ Snowball tab shows the debt snowball method — pay minimums on all, attack the smallest balance first. Great for motivation.
4. The 🌊 Avalanche tab shows the debt avalanche method — pay minimums on all, attack the highest interest rate first. Saves the most money.
5. The 💡 Extra Payment tab lets you simulate adding extra monthly dollars to see how much faster you pay off debt.

Tip: For credit cards, use the avalanche method if your highest rate is 20%+. The interest savings are significant.
Credit Score Hub — Track & Improve Your Credit

Key process flow:
1. Click + Log Scores to enter your current scores from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). Check these for free monthly at AnnualCreditReport.com.
2. The Overview tab shows your score history trend and what score range you're in (Poor / Fair / Good / Very Good / Exceptional).
3. Each bureau tab (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) shows detailed report data and lets you log disputes.
4. Click + Update Report Data to enter account counts, utilization, payment history, and other factors that influence your score.
5. To log a dispute, go to a bureau tab and click + Log Dispute. Track status (Pending → In Progress → Resolved/Denied).

Score improvement tips: Pay on time (35% of score), keep utilization under 30% (30%), avoid opening many new accounts (15%), maintain old accounts (15%).
Settings — Profile, Goals & Data Management

Profile: Enter your name and birth date. Set your goals in the Goals section — your #1 priority goal drives the Dashboard countdown. Income is tracked in the Bill Tracker (Income Sources and Log Income) and the Budget section.

Layout: Switch between Desktop (bottom nav with full labels, multi-column layout) and Mobile (bottom nav with short labels, single column, larger touch targets). The app auto-detects your device on first visit. The app auto-detects your device on every load — no manual override needed.

Financial Readiness Gauge: The gauge scores your financial health using your savings balance, debt load, credit score, and budget surplus. Add savings accounts via the Goals section to improve the savings component.

Data Management:
• Export All Data (JSON) — Saves a complete backup of everything in the app. Do this regularly!
• Import Data (JSON) — Restores a previous backup. Useful when moving to a new browser or device.
• Reset All Data — Permanently deletes everything. Always export first.

Important: All data is stored locally in your browser (localStorage). It does NOT sync across devices or browsers. Export regularly to avoid data loss.
